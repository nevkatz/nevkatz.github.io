Animating SVG Mouth Expressions with Greensock

Sep 17, 2019

Intro

Have you ever wanted to animate something on the web?

I had been thinking about this post for weeks. I had created an alien creature and after making a range of expressions, I wanted to simply make my alien creature go from a smile to a surprised expression.

Here is the completed animation, done with Greensock animation library.

Transition 1: We need a library

My first approach on a CodePen was using the setAttribute command in Javscript for the d attribute of the mouth path.

I had heard from Sara Soudaian and Sarah Drasner that animation libraries are key for complex animations, so after my first try didn’t work, I immediately went to try Greensock. The syntax is super easy. Here are the commands I tried:

  • Tweenlite - use for single simple animations
  • TweenMax - use for single robust animations (?)
  • TimelineMax - use for multiple animations

MorphSVG makes this easier, but it is not for free. You need to be a member of the “Shockingly Green” subscription, which is $100. For now, I’m fine with counting my nodes.

I had initially created a round mouth and got unsatisfactory results - GreenSock (again, without the paid-for MorphSVG) was unable to tween the transition. It either looked like a pretzel or the mouth disappeared.

What I ended up doing was back-tracking to the first mouth I drew in Inkscape and drawing a small mouth expression that was not as drastic a transition to the smile.

  • I kept the number of nodes the same
  • I avoided rotating the mouth; I only resized it
  • I would change the control points on the nodes, but I would not reorder the sequence of nodes at all
